Game Description

A collection of strange tales about a family in Washington state.

As Edith, you’ll explore the colossal Finch house, searching for stories as she explores her family history and tries to figure out why she’s the last one in her family left alive. Each story you find lets you experience the life of a new family member on the day of their death, with stories ranging from the distant past to the present day.

Game Tips

1. Play in a dark setting
  - There's nothing stopping you from playing in a dark setting, so that's what I recommend. It fuels the emotion in the game when you're in a
  dark setting without distractions.

2. Use headphones
  - The voice acting and ambience in the game is incredible. Using headphones just increases the immersion to Edith's voice and the world.

3. Go slow, and explore the world
  - The game is meant for you to explore the house and the stories, don't try to rush to the next story, it ruins the current story you're on.

4. Use your imagination
  - What Remains of Edith Finch is very metaphorical, so be prepared to walk away without a real answer, but an interpretive one, the game usually
 doesn't hand out answers.

5. Play in one sitting
  - It ruins the emotional experience if you don't play it all in one sitting, it's only about 2-3 hours, so plan out a 2-3 hour block of time you have
  and finish the game.

What I love about What Remains of Edith Finch

Edith Finch is a game that really makes you think, it uses metaphors and symbols to describe scenes. It's like a poem. The ending is super well done, tied up neatly with a nice bow. The atmosphere is also amazing, love the attention to detail with the world. Love the message that it brought to me, worth playing!

Parents' Guide

Death is a massive part in this game, it is extremely confronting in the way death is viewed. Blood is also present in the game. Drugs are also referenced.
