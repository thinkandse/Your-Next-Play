Game Tips

1. Have questions, but don't try to answer them.
  - Omori, especially at the start, is very dream-like and surreal. You'll have questions about the world, characters, and plot. Don't try to answer   them right away; instead, focus on experiencing the game and its atmosphere. The answers will come in time as you progress through the story.

2. Save often and in multiple slots.
  - Omori features multiple endings, if you want to experience those endings without restarting, get multiple save files. Save files are also good   for peace of mind in case you make a choice you regret or want to explore different paths.

3. Be curious.
  - The game is filled with secrets, hidden areas, and side quests. Omori loves to reward players who explore and interact with the world beyond the   main storyline. Talk to NPCs, investigate objects, and try different actions to uncover all the game has to offer.

4. Use emotions wisely.
  - The emotion system is a key gameplay mechanic in Omori. Different emotions affect your battle and can be the difference between winning and   losing, especially with harder bosses. Learn how each emotion works and strategize accordingly during battles.

5. Take a break if you need to.
  - Omori can be emotionally intense at times, especially towards the end. If you find yourself feeling overwhelmed, it's okay to take a break and   come back later with a fresh perspective.

6. Play in chunks of about 2-3 hours.
  - You don't want to burn yourself out on Omori, as it is a game that thrives on atmosphere and emotional engagement. Playing in shorter
  sessions allows you to absorb the story and world better without feeling fatigued. You also don't want to jump in for a short session, as you won't   experience much emotional impacts.

What I love about Omori

The Characters... I LOVE the characters so much! Their witty dialogue and cute personalities are so fun and match the lore so well! I don't have an issue with any character honestly, especially the main ones.

I also love the cute art of the game, the pixel style is just to die for... There's two main art styles in Omori, the pixel style where most of the world is set, and cutscene/character style, which has a more pencil and hand drawn feel. The pixel art style is honestly so easy on the eyes.

Lastly, the story. I love the story so much. The dream world doesn't take away from the story world at all, it's a fun way to look at the characters after the real world. The story is emotional, nostalgic, and heartwarming. Definitely a must play for anyone who loves story driven games.
